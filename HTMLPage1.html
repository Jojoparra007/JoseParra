Project 1

This project employs advanced Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) to simulate a two-dimensional mixing chamber driven by rotating disks. By solving the Navier-Stokes, continuity, and mass fraction transport equations, the study captures complex fluid flow and scalar distribution within the chamber. Utilizing high-precision numerical methods like WENO schemes for advection, Crank-Nicolson for diffusion, and multigrid solvers for pressure correction ensures accurate and stable results. Carefully defined boundary conditions replicate realistic inlet, outlet, and wall interactions. Key metrics such as kinetic energy and scalar mixing efficiency are analyzed to evaluate and optimize the chamber’s performance. The findings provide critical insights for enhancing mixing processes in applications like chemical reactors and environmental engineering, demonstrating the project's potential to improve industrial mixing efficiency and effectiveness.
